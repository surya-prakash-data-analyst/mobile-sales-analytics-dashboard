# Business Impact Analysis

## Revenue Performance

| Year | Revenue | Change |
|------|---------|--------|
| 2021 (Oct–Dec) | ₹58.8M | Partial year |
| 2022 | ₹262.0M | +345% (first full year) |
| 2023 | ₹253.3M | **–3.3%** |
| 2024 (Jan–Oct) | ₹195.1M | **–23% pace** |

The 2022 to 2023 decline of 3.3% is modest but directional. The 2024 pace suggests the decline is accelerating — potentially driven by increased competition from large e-commerce platforms, post-COVID consumer spending normalization, or inventory mix issues. Without resolving the root cause, the trend will continue.

## Revenue by Segment — Where the Money Comes From

### By City

Delhi and Mumbai together contribute 43% of total revenue. The remaining 57% is spread across many smaller cities, each contributing 3–4%.

| City | Revenue | % of Total |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Delhi | ₹203.9M | 26.5% |
| Mumbai | ₹127.2M | 16.5% |
| Top 2 combined | ₹331.1M | 43.0% |
| All other cities | ₹438.1M | 57.0% |

**Risk:** High geographic concentration in Delhi means any disruption (new competitor, logistics issue, economic shock) would disproportionately impact overall revenue.

### By Age Segment

| Age Group | Revenue | Share |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| 46–60 | ₹249.5M | 32.4% |
| 26–35 | ₹189.2M | 24.6% |
| 36–45 | ₹182.1M | 23.7% |
| 18–25 | ₹148.4M | 19.3% |

The 46–60 segment buying more premium devices is the highest-value cohort. At the same time, the 18–25 segment at 19.3% is the lowest — possibly reflecting affordability constraints that mid-range models partially address.

### By Brand

All five brands are within ₹18M of each other across three years. No brand has achieved a meaningful competitive advantage in this portfolio.

## Cost of Declining Revenue

If the 2024 trajectory continues into 2025 (annualizing 2024 pace):

- Projected 2025 revenue: ~₹195–210M (vs ₹262M peak in 2022)
- Revenue gap from peak: ~₹52–67M per year
- Over 3 years without intervention: **₹150–200M in lost revenue vs 2022 pace**
