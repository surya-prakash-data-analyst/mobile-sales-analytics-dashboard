# Executive Summary — Mobile Sales Analytics

**Project:** Mobile Sales Analytics Dashboard  
**Period Analyzed:** October 2021 – October 2024  
**Records:** 3,835 transactions · ₹769M total revenue

---

## The Situation

A mobile device retailer operating across multiple Indian cities needed to move from spreadsheet-based record-keeping to a structured analytics framework. Revenue data existed but was never analyzed for trends, segment performance, or growth opportunity. Leadership was making inventory and marketing decisions without data visibility.

---

## What the Data Shows

**Revenue is declining and the pace is accelerating.**
After growing massively from 2021 (partial year, ₹58.8M) to 2022 (₹262.0M), revenue declined 3.3% in 2023 to ₹253.3M. Through October 2024, revenue stands at ₹195.1M — on pace for a 23% annual decline. Understanding and reversing this trend is the most important business priority.

**The market is fragmented — no single brand dominates.**
All five brands sit within 12% of each other by revenue share: Apple (21.0%), Samsung (20.8%), OnePlus (20.0%), Vivo (19.5%), Xiaomi (18.7%). This tight competition means inventory and marketing decisions about brand mix matter — there is no safe bet to over-index on any single brand.

**Delhi is the engine of the business.**
Delhi contributes ₹203.9M — 26.5% of total revenue. Mumbai at ₹127.2M is the only other city above ₹100M. Every other city sits at ₹27–31M. The business is highly concentrated geographically.

**The 46–60 age segment is the most valuable customer group.**
This segment generated ₹249.5M — 32.4% of all revenue — making it the highest of any age group. These customers are likely buying premium and flagship devices. They deserve dedicated marketing attention.

**Mid-range models outsell flagships.**
iPhone SE at ₹59.6M leads all models, ahead of Galaxy Note 20 (₹56.0M) and Galaxy S21 (₹53.3M). Customers want brand recognition at accessible price points. Mid-range inventory should be prioritized.

**Customer satisfaction is good but not excellent.**
61.1% of customers rated their purchase 4 or 5 stars. The 38.8% who gave 1–3 stars represents a meaningful minority worth investigating through after-sales service review.

---

## Top 5 Recommendations

1. **Investigate the revenue decline** — Conduct a competitive pricing audit, analyze which brands/models lost share in 2023–24, and interview sales staff on what customers are saying.
2. **Invest more in Delhi** — The data clearly shows Delhi is the best market. More inventory, a dedicated sales team, and city-specific marketing would be high-ROI investments.
3. **Target the 46–60 segment** — Build a campaign specifically for this group: EMI financing on premium models, trade-in offers on older devices, and loyalty rewards.
4. **Expand mid-range stock** — iPhone SE and OnePlus Nord lead revenue. These models likely face demand that outpaces available inventory. Prioritize stocking mid-range SKUs across all cities.
5. **Address the satisfaction gap** — 38.8% of customers giving low ratings is a retention and word-of-mouth risk. A structured post-purchase follow-up process and better after-sales service would improve repeat purchase rates.
